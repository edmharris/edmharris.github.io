Life Experience

England

After a few early years living in the county of Kent, I grew up in Peterborough in the county of Cambridgeshire, east of England and lived there until I attended Lancaster University in the north-west at the age of 17. Following University, where I graduated with a degree in Philosophy and a Masters in Organizational Behaviour, I moved to London, the capital city of England, and lived there for 8 years before moving to Canada.

London is the most populous city in the United Kingdom, with a metropolitan area of over 13 million inhabitants.

Picture of London Bridge courtesy of Charles Postiaux on Unsplash.

Sweden

The maternal side of the family is from the southern most county of Skåne where the 16 km Øresund bridge connects Denmark with Sweden - Europe’s longest road and rail bridge. I lived in Gothenburg for the first six years of my life and have English / Swedish parentage.

Malmö is the capital city of Skåne county and the third-largest city in Sweden, after Stockholm and Gothenburg.

Picture of Sweden courtesy of Jessica Pamp on Unsplash.

Canada

I moved to Canada in 2006 in my early 30s and established a family with my then Spouse in the town of Stratford and have two wonderful children. Following our separation, I met my current Spouse and became based in a nearby Ontario town and enjoy all the wonderful aspects of blended family life.

Canada is an incredible country and I can't wait to continue to explore all that it has to offer.
